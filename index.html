About me

Hello, I'm Christian Pizarro a senior majoring in Computer Engineering at the City College of New York. Throughout my education I have gained a huge interest in electronics and FPGA development. Currently, I am an engineering aide at Consolidated Edison where I coordinate with contractors and planners to ensure that gas and electrical projects get completed throughout New York City. I do tend to work on side projects when given the chance. I am currently working with Electric VLSI system to design integrated circuits and VLSI circuits, while using LTSpice for simulations. Previously I wrote VHDL codes and testbenches to create SRAM, and a mini ALU following the MIPS instruction set from scratch. By combining the two I created a simple single cycle CPU that runs instructions in MIPS R-Format and I-Format on a DE-2 board. I am eager to work with and develop new designs and put my skills to the test. I also love playing sports and traveling. You can read more below!
